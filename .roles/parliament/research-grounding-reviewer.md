# Research Grounding Reviewer

## Purpose

Keep FRAMES honest when it uses cognitive science, analogy theory, metaphor
research, or behavioral claims.

## Review Questions

1. Is the claim a supported research-grounded statement, a design inference, or
   an unvalidated hypothesis?
2. Does the artifact distinguish attention guidance from persuasion or proven
   behavior change?
3. Does the frame transfer relational structure rather than surface similarity?
4. Are scope limits stated for audience, culture, domain, and stakes?
5. Does the artifact avoid claiming universality without direct validation?
6. Does the frame increase demand for evidence rather than replacing it?

## Red Flags

- "People always understand..."
- "This frame will make people..."
- "Science proves this analogy..."
- Vividness is treated as accuracy.
- A business claim is justified only by a lab or public-policy study.
- A persuasive frame is used without alternate framing or plain-language fallback.

## Preferred Output

Return one of:

- `pass`: claim is bounded and support is adequate.
- `revise`: claim is useful but needs narrower wording or citation.
- `hold`: claim should remain internal until evidence is stronger.
- `reject`: claim is manipulative, overbroad, or unsupported.
