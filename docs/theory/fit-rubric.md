# Fit Rubric

The fit rubric scores whether a frame is ready to use, revise, or reject.

Use this rubric after drafting the source scene, target situation, transfer map,
action cue, evidence boundary, and misuse warning.

## Scoring

Each dimension gets 0, 1, or 2 points.

| Score | Meaning |
|---|---|
| 0 | Missing, misleading, unsafe, or not usable. |
| 1 | Present but weak, vague, audience-limited, or needs revision. |
| 2 | Clear, useful, bounded, and ready for the intended audience. |

Maximum score: 14.

Use [story-job-taxonomy.md](story-job-taxonomy.md) after the base score when a
frame is being used as a narrative, example, leadership story, conflict-repair
story, teaching story, or persuasion-sensitive communication.
Use [relational-transfer-fields.md](relational-transfer-fields.md) when scoring
`Transfer clarity`; surface similarity is not enough.
Use [claim-strength-labels.md](claim-strength-labels.md) before presenting a
passing score as stronger than a heuristic.

## Dimensions

| Dimension | 0 | 1 | 2 |
|---|---|---|---|
| Recognition | Audience may not know the source scene. | Source is familiar to some readers but needs setup. | Source is immediately recognizable to the intended audience. |
| Target specificity | Target is generic or abstract. | Target is recognizable but broad. | Target is concrete enough to guide action. |
| Transfer clarity | Mapping is unstated or imports bad details. | Mapping is partial or too broad. | Mapping names exactly what transfers and what does not. |
| Action cue | No clear next move. | Suggests a direction but not a workflow move. | Names a clear next action, pause, decision, or escalation. |
| Evidence boundary | Frame stands in for proof. | Evidence need is implied but not explicit. | Says what still must be checked. |
| Human safety | Casts people as hazards, obstacles, or weak objects. | Avoids harm but has possible misuse risk. | Preserves agency and names protected values. |
| Stop condition | Frame can continue indefinitely. | Stop condition is implied. | Says when to stop, switch, or retire the frame. |

## Decision Bands

| Total | Decision | Meaning |
|---:|---|---|
| 12-14 | Accepted | Ready for catalog/index use with normal review. |
| 9-11 | Revise | Useful candidate, but fix weak dimensions first. |
| 6-8 | Hold | Keep as a research or draft note; do not index yet. |
| 0-5 | Reject | Too unclear, unsafe, or low-value. |

## Hard Stops

Reject or rewrite immediately if any hard stop appears:

- People are framed as obstacles, hazards, cargo, or weak actors.
- The frame hides who owns the decision.
- The frame replaces evidence, thresholds, or review.
- The frame pressures agreement instead of clarifying tradeoffs.
- The source scene is likely unfamiliar to the intended audience.
- The story layer claims to teach, repair, or warn while actually hiding motive,
  authority, evidence, or accountability.

## Story Job Overlay

The story-job overlay does not change the 14-point base score. It blocks
acceptance when the narrative purpose is unsafe or unclear.

| Overlay Check | Ready | Revise | Hold |
|---|---|---|---|
| Story-job clarity | Narrative purpose is explicit and matches the use case. | Purpose is implied but vague. | Claimed purpose conflicts with actual persuasive effect. |
| Audience-role alignment | Audience role, duty, and agency are fair. | Role is mostly fair but needs qualification. | Story assigns manipulative, false, or unsafe duty. |
| Vividness / evidence balance | Vividness helps recall while evidence stays visible. | Evidence boundary needs strengthening. | Story feels like proof. |
| Alternate readiness | Alternate story job exists for contested use. | Alternate exists but is not documented. | No alternate exists for medium- or high-stakes use. |

Any `Hold` result keeps the frame out of accepted catalog/index use for
medium- or high-stakes contexts.

## Worked Example: Four-Way Stop

Target situation: several peer teams need turn order around constrained
attention.

| Dimension | Score | Rationale |
|---|---:|---|
| Recognition | 2 | Familiar to many US driving audiences; may need alternate for non-driving audiences. |
| Target specificity | 2 | Peer teams and constrained attention are explicit. |
| Transfer clarity | 2 | Stop, read order, signal, proceed map cleanly. |
| Action cue | 2 | Pause, establish order, move visibly. |
| Evidence boundary | 1 | Needs explicit owner/authority check. |
| Human safety | 2 | Does not cast people as obstacles. |
| Stop condition | 1 | Needs clearer switch when authority is unequal. |

Total: 12. Decision: accepted with audience and authority warnings.

## Worked Example: Team as Roadblock

Target situation: one team is delaying another.

| Dimension | Score | Rationale |
|---|---:|---|
| Recognition | 2 | Easy to picture. |
| Target specificity | 1 | Delay is recognizable but owner/dependency is vague. |
| Transfer clarity | 0 | Imports obstacle/removal logic into people. |
| Action cue | 0 | Suggests routing around a team rather than resolving dependency. |
| Evidence boundary | 0 | Hides the actual decision or capacity evidence. |
| Human safety | 0 | Casts people as obstacles. |
| Stop condition | 0 | No safe stop condition. |

Total: 3. Decision: reject. Rewrite as blocked dependency, capacity limit, or
handoff failure.

## Review Workflow

1. Score independently before discussion.
2. Discuss any dimension with a 0.
3. Apply hard stops before averaging opinions.
4. Revise the frame entry.
5. Re-score only the changed dimensions.
6. Record accepted risks in the frame note or role review.
